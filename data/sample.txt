General Knowledge About India

India is a country located in South Asia and is officially known as the Republic of India.
It is the seventh-largest country in the world by land area and the most populous country as of recent estimates.
India shares its borders with Pakistan, China, Nepal, Bhutan, Bangladesh, and Myanmar.

The capital city of India is New Delhi.
India has 28 states and 8 union territories.
Each state has its own government, while union territories are governed directly by the central government.

India gained independence from British rule on 15 August 1947.
The first Prime Minister of independent India was Jawaharlal Nehru.
The Constitution of India came into effect on 26 January 1950, which is celebrated as Republic Day.

The Indian Parliament consists of two houses: the Lok Sabha and the Rajya Sabha.
The President of India is the constitutional head of the country, while the Prime Minister is the head of the government.

India has a diverse culture with multiple religions, languages, and traditions.
The Constitution recognizes 22 official languages, with Hindi and English being used for official government work.
Major religions practiced in India include Hinduism, Islam, Christianity, Sikhism, Buddhism, and Jainism.

The Indian economy is one of the largest in the world and is classified as a mixed economy.
Major sectors include agriculture, manufacturing, and services.
India is known for its information technology industry and is a global hub for software services.

Some major rivers of India include the Ganga, Yamuna, Brahmaputra, and Godavari.
The Himalayas lie in the northern part of India and play a crucial role in climate and river systems.

India is a democratic country and follows a parliamentary system of governance.
Elections are conducted by the Election Commission of India to choose representatives at various levels.
